India oh India. For approximately 12 years India was my home for the winter season. I had a hammock hanging between two coconut trees and my guitar. I would start every winter spending 3 weeks in a yoga ashram and then to the beach, where my coconut trees are waiting for me at the same spot in the jungle, where I was already familiar with two families of cobras, two families of monkeys and a few hawks that were nesting above my hammock.

Growing up in the middle east allowed me to be exposed to different styles of music - from Arabic music, to Greek, Gypsy, Balkan... What all of those have in common is a very uncommon (relatively to the west...) approach to rhythm. In this session with Iris - an amazing percussionist - I quickly improvised a short piece with a challenging rhythmic pattern and asked her to give her own interpretation of this rhythmic adventure.

POV: My cockpit.
The process of performing "It's Just A Muscle" - a run of over 130 shows (Hebrew and English versions combined) was a process of creating a language. Choosing the right order of songs, choosing the essential parts of my story that need to be shared in between the songs and also what sound quality do I want the show to have. I knew I wanted a "rhythm section", but with this show being so intimate, it felt like I NEED to be alone on the stage. So I ended up using my guitar case as a bass-drum, and the leg-bells - a gift from my older sister when she came back from a student exchange program in India - as my snare drum.
