What does promotion look like
This is the first time I've done an American tour and I likely don't know people in your community. That being said, this is a new thing for me to be exploring and I would love for us to work together. Aubrey (my [wo]manager) and I want to make it as easy as possible while also aknowledging that we're going to need your support in promoting. I imagine this will look different in each location and we look forward to talking with you about the best ways to reach your community. Aubrey will provide all promotional materials and is likely to be the person you're communicating with the most.

What kind of hospitality is excepted from me?
This show is very intimate and heart centered, therefore I usually like it to be as simple as possibe. In Hebrew we have a beautiful word: KIBBUD. The word KIBBUD comes from "honoring" and basically it means that the there might be a small bite available for them but mainly they are there for the show. There's no pressure for you to offer some big spread or anything.

Is it kids friendly?
This concert is not going to be entertaining to the average child. In this show I'm touching many painful topics; if you're open to have your kids asking you questions the day after the show, then they are welcome. The ideal scenario is for there to be a seperate space for children to play as they wish. We are also open to navigating this with you.
